## 01:30 - 02:20 - Scene 03: Airflow and Spin

Now watch the air.

With no spin, the flow is mostly symmetric. Air divides around the ball and the wake forms behind it.

Add spin, and that symmetry breaks.

The moving surface pulls the nearby air with it. The boundary layer changes. The wake behind the ball tilts.

That tilted wake is the clue. The ball has redirected air sideways, so the air has gained sideways momentum.

And momentum does not appear for free.

## 02:20 - 03:10 - Scene 04: The Magnus Force

If the ball pushes the air one way, the air pushes the ball the other way.

That reaction is the Magnus force.

It points sideways, perpendicular to the flight direction and tied to the spin axis. A compact way to remember the direction is omega cross v.

The important part is that this force is not a single kick in mid-air. It keeps acting while the ball is spinning and moving.

Tiny sideways pushes, over the whole flight, become a visible curve.
