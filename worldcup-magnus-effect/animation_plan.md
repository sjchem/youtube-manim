# Scene-by-Scene Animation Plan

Target length: about 7 minutes.

## Scene 01 - The Illusion

Length: about 45 seconds.

Open on a top-view free kick. The ball has just been struck. Freeze the moment, draw the straight path viewers expect, and show that it misses outside the post. Then draw the real curved path in green as the ball bends inside the goal. Very little text: "expected", "actual", and the core message.

Visual payoff: the mystery is visible before the explanation begins.

## Scene 02 - Off-Center Kick

Length: about 45 seconds.

Close-up on foot and ball. The contact point is offset from the center. A cyan velocity arrow and orange spin-axis arrow appear. The ball rotates rapidly. The equation `kick -> v + omega` forms below the ball.

Visual payoff: spin is not added later. It is hidden in the initial contact.

## Scene 03 - Airflow and Spin

Length: about 50 seconds.

Start with symmetric flow lines around a non-spinning ball. Replace the ball with a spinning one and bend the streamlines. Add an orange tilted wake behind the ball.

Visual payoff: the air is not passive; spin reorganizes it.

## Scene 04 - The Magnus Force

Length: about 50 seconds.

Show the spinning ball pushing the wake down and sideways. Then show the equal and opposite green force arrow on the ball. Introduce `F_M proportional to omega x v`.

Visual payoff: the sideways force is continuous and aerodynamic.

## Scene 05 - Curve Builder

Length: about 55 seconds.

Build the top-view path from a simple physics model. First show a dashed red no-Magnus reference. Then create the green simulated curve point by point. Add sparse Magnus force arrows along the path and the compact equation `m a = F_D + F_M + W`.

Visual payoff: many tiny sideways pushes become one dramatic curve.

## Scene 06 - Surface Matters

Length: about 50 seconds.

Use the supplied World Cup ball image if the local image stack can decode AVIF; otherwise use a vector montage from 1930 to 2026. Pull attention to seams, texture, and speed. Show a boundary-layer arrow skimming over a textured Trionda-like ball.

Visual payoff: ball design affects air, not just branding.

## Scene 07 - Trionda Design Lab

Length: about 55 seconds.

Show a tetrahedron-like geometric seed transforming into a four-panel Trionda-style ball. Beside it, draw a schematic drag-crisis graph with the Trionda marker near 43 km/h, based on the 2026 wind-tunnel reference. Notes appear: "roughness moves transition" and "wake controls force".

Visual payoff: panel geometry and roughness are aerodynamic design variables.

## Scene 08 - Final Synthesis

Length: about 55 seconds.

Layer the whole explanation: off-center kick, spin, tilted wake, side force, curved path. The ball finishes inside the goal. End on the single sentence: "A free kick curves because spin turns air into an invisible steering force."

Visual payoff: the mystery resolves into one compact causal chain.

## Scene 09 - Subscribe

Length: about 10 seconds.

Fade to a dedicated closing card with a large, mobile-readable subscribe prompt and a short curiosity-focused sign-off.

Visual payoff: the science story resolves before the channel call to action appears.
