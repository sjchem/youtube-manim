## Description

How can a football be kicked outside the goal and still bend back inside the post?

This animation explains the Magnus effect through one cinematic free kick. We start with the illusion of the straight path, then reveal the hidden spin, the airflow around the ball, the tilted wake, and the sideways aerodynamic force that keeps steering the ball through the air.

The 2026 World Cup Trionda ball is the modern hook: its four-panel design, seams, texture, and aerodynamics show why football construction affects flight, not just appearance.

## Pinned Comment

The key idea: spin does not "pull" the ball by itself. Spin changes the airflow and wake; the air pushes back sideways. That continuous sideways push is the Magnus force.

## Short Social Post

A curving free kick is not magic. The player gives the ball side-spin, the spinning surface bends the wake, and the air pushes the ball sideways all the way to goal. That is the Magnus effect.
