## 01:10-02:35 - Scene 02: Multiplication As Motion

Multiplication can be seen as repeated motion. Imagine a point on a number line, ready to jump.

Three times four means move four steps to the right, three times. The jumps land at four, eight, and twelve. So the answer is positive twelve.

Now change the direction. Three times negative four means move four steps to the left, three times. The jumps land at negative four, negative eight, and negative twelve.

So multiplication can describe movement. Positive steps point right. Negative steps point left. The sign is telling us the direction of the motion.

Visual notes: glowing particle jumps along the number line. Rightward motion is cyan. Leftward motion is red.

Music mood: clear rhythmic pulses for each jump.

## 02:35-04:05 - Scene 03: Negative Means Reversal

A negative sign is more than a small dash. It is an instruction to reverse.

Suppose positive four is an arrow pointing right. Multiply by negative one, and the size stays four, but the direction flips. Right becomes left. That is negative four.

Now multiply by negative one again. The arrow flips a second time. Left becomes right again.

That is the heart of the idea. One negative reverses direction. Two negatives reverse twice. And two reversals bring us back to the positive direction.

Visual notes: arrow rotates 180 degrees twice, with a circular guide and glow trail.

Music mood: airy turn sound for each reversal.

## 04:05-05:20 - Scene 04: The Mirror Room

Another way to feel this is to think of a negative sign as a mirror.

One mirror flips orientation. What pointed one way now appears reversed. But a second mirror flips the reflection again.

So two mirrors do not create a random new direction. They reverse the reversal.

That is what two negatives are doing. The first negative turns the idea around. The second negative turns it around again. The final direction is positive.

Visual notes: arrow passes through two glowing mirror planes and ends pointing the original way.

Music mood: reflective shimmer, then small resolution.

## 05:20-06:55 - Scene 05: Pattern Through Zero

Patterns also force the rule.

Look at products with negative four. Three times negative four is negative twelve. Two times negative four is negative eight. One times negative four is negative four. Zero times negative four is zero.

Now watch the pattern. As the first number goes down by one, the answer goes up by four: negative twelve, negative eight, negative four, zero.

So below zero, the pattern must continue. Negative one times negative four becomes four. Negative two times negative four becomes eight. Negative three times negative four becomes twelve.

If we made those answers negative, the pattern would suddenly break at zero. To keep arithmetic consistent, negative times negative must become positive.

Visual notes: table continues through zero, question marks fill with `+4`, `+8`, `+12`. A graph confirms the same smooth line.

## 06:55-08:55 - Scene 06: Distributive Law Machine

Now comes the deeper reason.

Mathematics refuses to break the distributive law. That law says multiplying by a sum should match multiplying each part separately and then adding.

Start with something simple: five plus negative five is zero. So negative three times that whole quantity must also be zero.

Now distribute. Negative three times five gives negative fifteen. Then we still have negative three times negative five.

The total must stay zero. So what number has to be added to negative fifteen? Positive fifteen.

That means negative three times negative five is forced to be positive fifteen. We did not pick the answer. The structure of arithmetic picked it.

Visual notes: expression enters a machine, splits into two products, then becomes a balance-scale equation. The unknown opens as `+15`.

Music mood: mechanical precision, warm reveal.

## 08:55-10:10 - Scene 07: Broken Universe

Now imagine we tried a different rule. Suppose negative times negative stayed negative.

At first, that might sound possible. But test it inside the same expression.

Five plus negative five is zero, so negative three times that whole quantity must be zero.

But after distributing, negative three times five is negative fifteen. And under the wrong rule, negative three times negative five would also be negative fifteen.

Negative fifteen plus negative fifteen is negative thirty. So the same expression would equal zero and negative thirty.

That is a contradiction. The distributive law would fail, and the number system would stop being consistent.

These are different windows into one idea: negative times negative must be positive, or the number system breaks.
