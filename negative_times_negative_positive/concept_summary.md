# Scientific Concept Summary

Multiplication by a negative number can be understood as reversal: it changes direction on the number line and flips the sign of a quantity. A single reversal turns positive direction into negative direction. A second reversal turns it back.

That intuition is supported by arithmetic structure. Multiplication patterns must continue smoothly through zero, so the sequence

```text
3 × (-4) = -12
2 × (-4) = -8
1 × (-4) = -4
0 × (-4) = 0
-1 × (-4) = 4
```

is forced by the constant change of `+4` in the product.

The rigorous reason is the distributive law. Since `5 + (-5) = 0`, the expression `-3(5 + (-5))` must equal `0`. But distributing gives `(-3)(5) + (-3)(-5) = -15 + (-3)(-5)`. For the total to remain zero, `(-3)(-5)` must be `+15`.

So `(-a)(-b)=ab` is not an arbitrary rule. It is the only choice that keeps the number line, patterns, additive inverses, and distributive law consistent.
