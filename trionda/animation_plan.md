# Scene-by-Scene Animation Plan

## Scene 1: Cutaway Hook

Open on a glowing TRIONDA-like ball. A bright cutter sweeps through it. The ball shifts aside and a cutaway appears: outer cover, support layers, bladder, and a side-mounted sensor. End with the promise: not just fewer panels, but a precision aerodynamic instrument.

## Scene 2: Outer Skin and Material System

Show the surface as a layered stack: textured polymer cover, graphics/icons, support/backing layers, bladder. Animate airflow over the cover and a boot contacting the surface. The narration avoids claiming an exact chemical formula.

## Scene 3: Thermal Bonding vs Stitching

Split screen: old stitched construction on the left, thermally bonded construction on the right. Animate heat joining panel edges. Emphasize seam depth, water resistance, and repeatability.

## Scene 4: Panel Geometry

Transform from an older many-panel map to a four-panel map. Show a tetrahedron idea and then a deep, wavy seam sample with debossed texture. Key visual message: fewer panels alone is not the whole trick.

## Scene 5: Boundary Layer

Compare a smooth sphere and a textured ball. Airflow separates early around the smooth sphere, creating a larger unstable wake. Engineered roughness delays separation and produces a more controlled wake.

## Scene 6: Drag Crisis Graph

Show a drag coefficient versus speed graph. A ball marker moves through a sudden transition. Introduce the drag equation visually and emphasize "predictable drag" rather than "minimum drag."

## Scene 7: Sensor Inside

Return to the cutaway. Zoom to the side-mounted chip layer. Show counter-balances across other panels. Animate 500 Hz pulses and route data toward VAR.

## Scene 8: AI + VAR Fusion

Build a small pitch diagram with passer, attacker, defender, ball, offside line, tracking dots, and ball-touch pulse. Data streams into a panel: ball touch time, player positions, AI alignment. End synthesis: materials + seams + air + data = TRIONDA.

## Scene 9: Subscribe Card

Separate closing subscribe card matching the requested structure.
